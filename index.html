Cybersecurity Basics

Cybersecurity is about protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. As a student or everyday user, you can reduce risk by following simple habits and staying alert to scams.

Core Principles

  • Confidentiality — keep data private.
  • Integrity — prevent unauthorized changes.
  • Availability — ensure systems are up when needed.

Good Digital Hygiene

  • Use strong, unique passwords.
  • Turn on multi-factor authentication (MFA).
  • Update software and apps promptly.
  • Back up important files regularly.

Common Threats

Fraudulent messages pretending to be trusted sources to steal logins or money.

  • Look for mismatched URLs, urgency, grammar mistakes, or unexpected attachments.
  • Verify using official channels (don’t use the links in the message).

Malicious software (viruses, ransomware, spyware) that can damage or lock your files.

  • Download only from trusted stores/sources.
  • Keep OS and antivirus up to date.

Manipulating people to bypass security (pretexting, baiting, tailgating).

  • Slow down, verify identity, and follow procedures.

Unencrypted networks can expose your traffic to eavesdropping.

  • Avoid sensitive logins on open Wi‑Fi; use VPN where possible.

Protect Yourself

Strong Passwords & MFA

Use at least 12–16 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Prefer a passphrase you can remember, and enable MFA everywhere.

We never send your input anywhere.

Safe Browsing Checklist

  • Check the URL (beware typos, extra characters, odd domains).
  • Look for HTTPS (padlock) but also verify the site name.
  • Don’t reuse passwords across accounts.
  • Keep OS/browser/plugins updated.
  • Back up files (e.g., cloud + external drive).

Quick Phishing Quiz

Pick the safest action in each scenario.

1) You receive an email “Your account will be closed in 24 hours! Click here.”
2) A friend sends a file you weren’t expecting via DM.
3) Public Wi‑Fi at a café asks you to log into your email.

Trusted Resources

  • Tip: If you suspect phishing, report to your email provider and your campus IT/security team.
